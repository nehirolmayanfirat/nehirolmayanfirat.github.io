01. About Me

Hello! I live in the city of Giresun, Turkey. For years, I have tried to gain experience by experimenting with various Linux distro's, desktop environments, and window managers.

The Origin of My Curiosity

The Spark of Curiosity

It all started with a simple question: "How do these websites work?". I began to research the technology behind the pages that appeared when I typed an address into the browser, driven by pure curiosity.

Discovering Web Hosting

My research led me to the concept of “web hosting.” I learned what people use to host websites and also about domain names. This idea made sense, but it also brought up a new question.

"How Do They Provide It?"

So, how did the person offering this service do it? I researched it and decided to try providing it myself, so I got a VDS (virtual private server). To be honest, I had bought this server to learn web hosting, but it served another purpose, and I was very amateurish! I had set up a desktop environment on the server, yes... it was embarrassing, but in the end, I learned a lot..

A 'Virtual' Server Must Have a Physical One, Right?

Learning that a computer could be virtually partitioned and rented out to multiple people was fascinating. I couldn't help but wonder, "Is there a level above this?". This curiosity led me to the concept of a 'Dedicated Server'.

So where are these located?

AAt this point, everything came together. I learned that virtual and private servers are housed in ‘Data Centers’ with their own dedicated cooling systems, electrical infrastructure, and massive internet connections. .
